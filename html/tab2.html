Blueshift

Blue shift is the displacement towards shorter wavelengths of the spectral lines of a light source. This effect occurs when the source is approaching an observer and is due to the Doppler effect. In the astronomical context, blue shift indicates that the light source is moving towards us, which can occur when a star or a galaxy is moving towards us. An example is:
Quasars: Quasars are extremely luminous astronomical objects located at the center of some galaxies. Because they are very far away from us, quasars exhibit a very high red shift in their spectrum. However, some quasars exhibit blue shifts, suggesting that they are closer to us than originally thought.
